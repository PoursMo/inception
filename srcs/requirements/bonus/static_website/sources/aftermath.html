Aftermath

The battle created the Saint-Mihiel salient projecting into the French lines south of Verdun. Of the two roads and one railway that led to Verdun, only a minor road remained open, which imposed severe supply difficulties on the French troops in the RFV. The city was enveloped on three sides and neutralised as a base for French offensive operations. The area would see much fighting over the rest of the war.
